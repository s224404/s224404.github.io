The Historical Peak

In the late 2000s, San Francisco witnessed a significant surge in narcotics-related incidents. This era defined the baseline for drug crime in the city, with traditional enforcement and reporting reaching an all-time high in the years leading up to 2010.

The Pandemic Silence

The global pandemic brought the city to a standstill. In 2021, reported incidents reached an unprecedented low. While the data suggests a decline, it likely masks a shift from public street-level activity to more secluded environments during the lockdowns.

A Dangerous Resurgence

Since 2022, the trend has reversed with alarming speed. The resurgence observed from 2022 to 2025 indicates a new phase of the crisis. With synthetic opioids like Fentanyl entering the market, the city is facing its most lethal challenge in decades.
