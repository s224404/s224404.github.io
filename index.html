The 2011 Surge

Over a decade ago, San Francisco reached a critical threshold. Law enforcement recorded a massive peak in narcotics-related incidents, particularly in the Tenderloin district. The city was facing a wave of traditional drug offenses that defined a generation of crime data.

The Pandemic Silence

As the world stood still in 2020, so did the streets of San Francisco. Reported incidents plummeted to historical lows. While the data suggests a city at rest, experts argue the markets merely moved indoors, hidden from the eyes of public records.

A Dangerous Resurgence

The post-pandemic era has introduced a new, lethal dynamic. From 2022 to 2025, we observe a steady and alarming increase. With the emergence of synthetic opioids like Fentanyl, the city is not just returning to old patterns—it is facing an entirely new crisis.

Data Concludes...

This analysis reveals that narcotics crime in San Francisco is not static. It reacts to global events, policy shifts, and the evolving nature of the substances themselves. Understanding this data is the first step toward finding a path forward.
