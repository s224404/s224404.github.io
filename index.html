Historical Peak

The data shows a clear peak in drug and narcotic arrests in the year 2008, followed by a steady decline in the years after. This may be due to a shift toward treatment and harm reduction rather than punishment, along with declining trends in drugs like heroin and cocaine and thus fewer overall arrests.

The Pandemic Silence

The sharp drop during the COVID-19 pandemic is likely due to lockdowns and reduced social activity, which led to fewer police interactions and arrests. At the same time, law enforcement priorities shifted, meaning fewer drug-related offenses were recorded, even though drug use may not have decreased.

Post-Pandemic Boom

After the pandemic, drug and narcotic offenses begin to rise steadily again. This increase may reflect a return to normal social activity, more active policing, and the growing impact fentanyl boom all over the US.
