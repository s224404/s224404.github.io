The Historic Peak

Long before the current crisis, San Francisco faced an earlier wave of narcotics crime. Using automated data analysis, we identified the historical peak. During this era, traditional drug offenses dominated the police logs, reflecting a city struggling with a different kind of street-level market.

The Pandemic Silence

As the city enforced strict lockdowns in 2020 and 2021, reported crimes reached an unprecedented low. The streets were empty, and the markets appeared to vanish. However, this statistical dip didn't signal the end of the crisis—it only masked a shift in how and where these activities occurred.

A Modern Resurgence

Since 2022, the trend has reversed with alarming speed. The resurgence from 2022 to 2025 highlights a dangerous new reality. With the influx of synthetic opioids like Fentanyl, the data shows a city not just returning to old levels, but entering a more lethal phase of the crisis.

Reflecting on the Data

By visualizing these shifts, we see that crime is never just a number. It is a reflection of a city's pulse, reacting to global pandemics, shifting policies, and the evolving nature of the crisis itself.
